Current Position

UCSD Moshiri Lab

Undergraduate Researcher

The Moshiri Lab is a research lab run by professor Niema Moshiri at UCSD.
We focus on computational biology, particularly in the areas of HIV phylogenetics and molecular epidemiology.

  • First author on 2020 preprint ”An Evaluation of Phylogenetic Workflows in Viral Molecular Epidemiology” uploaded to bioRxiv and currently being peer reviewed for publication in the Journal of Molecular Biology and Evolution
  • You can see the repository with all the simulated sequence data and corresponding phylogenetic inference workflows here
  • Led the creation of quantitative metrics to evaluate the effectiveness of novel epidemiological tools and discovered an alternate workflow that reduces computation time by over 95% with no appreciable accuracy loss
  • Developing a web application with ReactJS and JavaScript to perform transmission clustering analyses on newick trees
